A Standard Guitar

Neck

  • typically consists of a fingerboard (or fretboard) where the frets are located, allowing players to press down on the strings to create different notes.

Guitar Box

  • hollow part of the guitar that serves as the base and also play an important role in the acoutiscs.

Frets

  • Frets are metal strips embedded in the fretboard of a guitar, and they play a crucial role in determining the pitch of the notes played.

Headstock

  • The headstock is the top part of the guitar neck, where the tuning pegs are located. It is typically shaped to provide balance and aesthetic appeal. The headstock's primary function is to anchor the strings and facilitate tuning.
